Personal History

Claude Bétrisey is originally from Switzerland, born and raised in the heart of Valais in the village of Saint-Léonard, famous for its underground lake, a popular tourist attraction.

He studied computer science at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Lausanne (EPFL), located on the scenic shores of Lake Geneva. There, he completed his Ph.D. thesis on digital typography, focusing on automatic hint generation for typographic characters based on a topological model.

In 1993, Claude moved to the United States to join Microsoft, where he contributed to groundbreaking work in digital typography, text rendering, and software engineering for over three decades. During his time at Microsoft, he met his wife Gloria. Together, they have one daughter, Paloma.

Paloma was the first in the family to move to the Palouse region, attending Washington State University (WSU) to study veterinary medicine. There, she met her husband, Mathew, and they now have a son, Maverick—Claude and Gloria's beloved grandchild.

Early in the pandemic, Claude and Gloria relocated to the Palouse to be closer to family, settling in Pullman, WA. In 2025, Claude joined Schweitzer Engineering Laboratories (SEL), a leading local company, where he continues to apply his expertise in software engineering and DevOps.
